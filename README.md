This study presents a recognition network architecture designed to identify landslide-induced road damage areas, which are categorized into three classes: intact roads, partially buried roads, and fully buried roads. The background is motivated by the fact that landslides triggered by earthquakes and heavy rainfall often result in road burial, causing physical road blockages and complete disruption of transportation networks. Such disasters not only pose severe threats to lives and property but also significantly hinder emergency rescue operations. The proposed network is developed to address three core challenges in automated recognition: 1. severe interference from complex backgrounds; 2. difficulty in adapting to multi-scale feature variations; 3. inadequate characterization of the spatial interaction between linear roads and regional landslide bodies.

The dataset containing data on road burial caused by landslides can be found at the following link: https://github.com/fenglanyue618-ops/landslide_road.git
